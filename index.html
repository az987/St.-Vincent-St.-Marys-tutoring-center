Children's After School Tutoring Program

A community-based, individualized after-school education program for children in grades 1-6, designed for year-round reading and math skill building to help bridge the educational gap.

Register Now

How Are We Different?

We differ from traditional classroom settings in a variety of ways:

Assessment

Children will be assessed in reading and math levels upon enrollment, enabling our tutors and staff to focus on areas of need for each child.

Personalized Attention

Unlike a typical classroom setting, our tutors are able to spend time with students to help them with their individual needs and identify early challenges that may hinder their educational development.

Engaging Environment

Our space allows students to talk, laugh and move around, as opposed to staying still in a classroom setting. This provides a break from their regular school day and helps them refocus.

Healthy Meals

A nutritious meal is provided to each child to improve concentration and ensure they have the energy to learn effectively.

Reward System

Students earn "Learning Points" to shop at our Reward Store, providing incentive to participate on a daily basis and recognize their achievements.

STEM Activities

Students ages 8-10 who are interested can participate in our robotics team, learning valuable STEM skills while having fun.

Program Facts:

  • Consistent attendance is mandatory, 2 days a week.
  • Sessions are Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday, from 4pm-6pm.
  • Enrollment is available for four different class sessions: Winter, Spring, Summer and Fall.
  • Students must demonstrate the ability to function positively in a group setting.
  • The program is completely FREE for eligible families.

As with adults, children also are affected by barriers to education. Some of their barriers can be directly attributed to the barriers of the adults surrounding them. Our program works to address these challenges and create positive learning experiences.

Program Requirements

Basic Requirements

  • First grade through sixth grade.
  • Please register early. Space is limited and enrollment is on a first-come, first-served basis.
  • FREE: No cost for the program, but 2 days a week attendance is mandatory.
  • Transportation to and from the Center is not provided.
  • Children are provided a meal each day.

Child Eligibility

Parent can answer yes to the following statements to determine basic eligibility:

  • My child enjoys reading and being read to.
  • My child would benefit from individualized reading and math assistance.
  • My child demonstrates the ability to function positively in individual and group settings.

Parental Support

Mandatory parental involvement includes:

  • Assuring consistent attendance.
  • Encouraging and supporting student participation and learning.
  • Willingness to communicate regularly with staff.

Top Five Barriers For Children's Education

Fear

Afraid of failing, making mistakes, what others might say.

Self-Confidence / Self-Esteem

Children have an "I can't" attitude that prevents them from trying.

Environment

No role models, education not a priority, living below poverty level, parents are not their first teacher.

Lack of Educational Support

Parents struggle and are unable to assist their children with homework, reading, math, etc.

Labels

Children are called negative names that affect their self-perception and academic confidence.
